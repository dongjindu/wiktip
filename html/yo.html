[edit] English

Wikipedia has an article on:
Yo

Wikipedia

[edit] Pronunciation

  • (UK) IPA: /jəʊ/, X-SAMPA: /j@U/
  • Rhymes: -əʊ
  • (US) IPA: /joʊ/, X-SAMPA: /joU/
  • Homophone: yo

[edit] Interjection

yo

  1. (slang) A greeting similar to hi.
    Yo Paulie! How's it going?
  2. (slang) An interjection similar to hey.
    Yo, check this out!
  3. (military slang) Present! Here!
    Sergeant: Smith? Private Smith: Yo!

[edit] Contraction

yo

  1. (African American Vernacular) contraction of you and are
    Yo a fool.

[edit] Pronoun

yo

  1. (colloquial) shortened form of your.
    Yo sandwich only has bacon in it. Want some ketchup on that?
  2. (Baltimore) third-person singular, familiar
    Yo was tuckin' in his shirt! (Stotko and Troyer 2007)

[edit] Synonyms

  • (greeting): hey, hi; see also Category:Greetings
  • (interjection): hey

[edit] Spanish

[edit] Etymology

From Vulgar Latin *eo, attested from the 6th century in Romance, from Latin ego.; akin to Greek εγώ, Sanskrit aham, all from Proto-Indo-European *éǵh₂.

[edit] Pronunciation

  • IPA: [ʝo̞]
  • IPA: [ɟʝo̞]

[edit] Pronoun

yo

  1. First-person singular pronoun in the nominative case. I.

[edit] See also

    Spanish pronouns

First person:

  • singular: yo, mí, me
  • plural: nosotros, nosotras, nos

Second person:

  • tuteo: tú, ti, te
  • voseo: vos, te
  • formal: usted, ustedes, le, les
  • familiar plural: vosotros, vosotras, ustedes, os, les

Third person:

  • masculine: él, ellos, lo, le, los, les
  • feminine: ella, ellas, la, le, las, les
  • neuter: ello, lo, le

Demonstrative:

  • este, esta, esto, estos, estas
  • ese, esa, eso, esos, esas
  • aquel, aquella, aquello, aquellos, aquellas

[edit] Usage notes

  • When more pronouns are included in the same sentence, it is considered impolite to say the pronoun yo at first; it must be the last one, and tú must be said after absent (this applies also for ti and mí):
    • Iremos Rosa, tú y yo, "Rosa, you and I will go"

[edit] Noun

yo m. (plural yos)

  1. In psychoanalysis, Freud's concept of the ego.
