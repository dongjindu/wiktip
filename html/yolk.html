[edit] Alternative forms

  • yelk (obsolete)

[edit] Etymology

From Old English ġeolca, from ġeolu (“yellow”).

[edit] Pronunciation

  • (RP) IPA: /jəʊk/, X-SAMPA: /j@UK/
  • Rhymes: -əʊk
  • (US) IPA: /joʊk/, /joʊlk/, X-SAMPA: /joUk/, /joUlk/
  • Audio (US)
    		(file)
  • Homophone: yoke

[edit] Noun

yolk (plural yolks)

  1. The yellow, spherical part of an egg that is surrounded by the white albumen, and serves as nutriment for the growing young.
    To make meringue, you have to separate the white from the yolk.
