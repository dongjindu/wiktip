[edit] Pronoun

your possessive

  1. Belonging to you; of you; related to you (singular; one owner).
    Let's meet tomorrow at your convenience.
    Is this your cat?
  2. Belonging to you; of you; related to you (plural; more owners).
  3. A determiner that conveys familiarity and mutual knowledge of the modified noun.
    Not your average Tom, Dick and Harry.
    Your Show of Shows
    Your World with Neil Cavuto
    Not Your Average Travel Guide

[edit] Usage notes

  • In US English, /j…ö/ is generally the unstressed version of /j…îÀê…π/; in many dialects, however, /j…ù/ is frequent even in positions of stress.
  • The use of your instead of you're is a common mistake in English.
