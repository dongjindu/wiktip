[edit] English

[edit] Pronunciation

  • (UK) IPA: /jɛn/, X-SAMPA: /"jEn/
  • Rhymes: -ɛn

[edit] Etymology 1

From Japanese 円 (en, “yen, circle”).

[edit] Noun

yen (plural yen)

  1. The unit of Japanese currency, equal to 100 sen.
  2. A coin or note worth one yen.

[edit] Etymology 2

From yen-yen (“a craving for opium”), from Mandarin 瘾 (Mandarin: yǐn; Cantonese: yan5, “addiction, craving (for opium)”).

[edit] Noun

yen (plural yens)

  1. A strong desire, urge, or yearning.
[edit] Synonyms
  • craving, desire, hankering, urge, yearning
[edit] Translations
strong desire
  • Dutch: verlangen (nl) n., hartstocht (nl) f.
  • Spanish: deseo (es) m., ganas (es) f. pl.

[edit] Verb

yen (third-person singular simple present yens, present participle yenning, simple past and past participle yenned)

  1. To have a strong desire for.
[edit] Translations
have strong desire for
  • Dutch: hartstochtelijk (nl) verlangen (nl), smachten (nl)
  • Italian: bramare (it), desiderare (it)
  • Spanish: desear (es)
