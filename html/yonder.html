[edit] English

[edit] Pronunciation

  • (RP) IPA: /ˈjɒndə(ɹ)/
  • (US) IPA: /ˈjɑndə(ɹ)/
    help
  • Rhymes: -ɒndə(r)

[edit] Etymology

Cognate with Dutch ginder ('over there').

[edit] Adverb

yonder (not comparable)

  1. In a distant, indicated place; over there.
    Whose home is that yonder?

[edit] Determiner

yonder

  1. Distant but within sight
    Yonder peasant - who is he?

[edit] Synonyms

  • (distant but within sight): yon

[edit] Translations

distant but within sight
  • Albanian: atje (sq)
  • Catalan: aquell (ca) m., aquella (ca) f.
  • Finnish: tuo (fi)

[edit] Noun

yonder (plural yonders)

  1. Something that is distant but within sight.
    Off we go, into the wild blue yonder, riding high into the sky.

[edit] Translations

something that is distant but within sight
  • Finnish: tuo (fi)
  • Japanese: あれ (are)

[edit] Usage notes

The term yonder is used more often in the South than elsewhere in the US.
