[edit] Etymology

Wikipedia has an article on:
Yes and no

Wikipedia

From Middle English ya, ye, from Old English ġēa (“yes”), from Proto-Germanic *ja (“yes”), from Proto-Indo-European *yē (“already”). Cognate with Dutch ja, German ja, Danish ja, Bokmål ja, New Norwegian ja and Swedish ja. Related to existing English terms yes and yea.

[edit] Pronunciation

[edit] Adverb

yeah (not comparable)

  1. (informal) Yes.
